Description:

General knowledge quizzes are ten a penny. Trust The Economist, which knows both the price and the value of everything, to do something different.

In its first ever quiz book in a 175-year history, the sharp wits of The Economists own champion quiz team (Marginal Futility) throw down the gauntlet for a genuinely severe contest. Ranging over the globe and the sweep of world history, peering into the most significant developments in science, politics and culture, this is the rare quiz whose answers shed real light on the ways of the world. What was Europes first attempt at an international currency union, before the Euro was a twinkle in Jacques Delorss eye? Where did 15th-Century popes live? Who sang Diggi-Loo Diggi-Ley?....
